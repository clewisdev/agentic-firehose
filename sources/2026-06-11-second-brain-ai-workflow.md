## Summary

Mike Jones reflects on integrating AI into a personal knowledge management system built on a decade of hand-written notes, Obsidian-based concept capture, and structured daily processing. The core insight: **distinguishing between vicious friction (taxes, busywork) and virtuous friction (the struggle that creates learning)** is essential when automating workflows.

Jones structures his AI-augmented system in three layers:

1. **What I know**: ~10 years of reading, thinking, and connections externalized in Obsidian—tagged concepts, linked ideas, Maps of Content organized by domain (organizational design, strategy, psychology, knowledge management).

2. **How I act**: AI "skills" that encode his work processes. Two key examples:
   - A skill that processes handwritten iPad notes (rapid logging) → parses, categorizes, routes tasks/notes/1–1 items to the vault. Pure vicious friction removal.
   - A skill for strategy drafting that assembles structure from notes + learnings + transcripts, *but* Jones retains thinking and judgment. Processing is offloaded; judgment is not.

3. **Where I am**: State aggregation (Oura, Withings, Strava, Substack stats + journal-derived emotional/focus signals). Answers: "Is what I'm doing working?"

The system is iterative, drawing from OODA loops (Boyd) and ReAct (agents). AI accelerates iteration speed but doesn't eliminate human involvement in learning. The caution: outsourcing virtuous friction (messy work where ideas stick) erodes the struggle that produces growth.

## Key Quotes

> "Outsourcing vicious friction frees capacity, but outsourcing virtuous friction means losing the struggle that makes you better."

> "Using this approach to note-taking means I have a structured system that provides a version of my taste for AI to draw on, as I would."

> "What AI changes for people is the speed of iteration, not the necessity of human involvement. No matter how efficient AI becomes, the important, messy learning remains with me."

> "Learning to adapt yourself to get the best out of the technology is a fascinating approach... Getting better means iterating on yourself."

## Takeaways

- **Friction taxonomy matters**: Vicious (busywork) vs. virtuous (learning-generative struggle). Automate the first; protect the second.
- **AI as processor, not decider**: Offload processing (assembly, routing, parsing), retain judgment and thinking.
- **Structured externalization enables AI leverage**: A decade of tagged, linked concept notes gives AI a "taste" to draw on; unstructured notes limit what AI can do.
- **State visibility closes the loop**: Aggregating performance signals (health, output, emotional/focus state) is how you know if the system is actually working.
- **Iteration speed, not elimination of human cognition**: AI accelerates OODA/ReAct loops but doesn't replace the human role in synthesizing and learning.
