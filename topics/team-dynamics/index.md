# Team dynamics

How AI tools change team behavior, incentive structures, and code quality discipline — who absorbs the cleanup cost of AI-generated "slop PRs" and how leadership must adapt.

## Sources

- [Building OpenCode with Dax Raad](../../sources/2026-06-02-opencode-podcast-dax-raad.md) — motivated engineers become cleanup crew for colleagues' unvetted AI code; engineers naturally "cash out" AI gains as time savings not increased output; CFO sales narratives vs. actual incentive structures are misaligned.
- [Is this sustainable? (Hurst)](../../sources/2026-06-09-ai-sustainable-hurst.md) — the "senior engineer tax" from the inside: AI expanded senior roles (more hands-on building *and* more strategy *and* more SME meetings), so the non-compressible human work — 1-2-1s, mentoring, thinking time — gets cut first because it's "invisible on a dashboard." Productivity gains captured by expanded expectations, not relief.
- [We don't write code anymore (Quigley/Sanity)](../../sources/2025-06-13-we-dont-write-code-anymore.md) — EM field report: senior team (10+ yrs each) now writes ~1% of code by hand; review discipline becomes the critical bottleneck; smaller teams ship more but only if they sustain the review/judgment load. Needs different hiring and management mental models. "Intent === ownership."
- [What Happens to Platform Teams?](../../sources/2026-06-15-platform-teams-ai-era.md) — demo-first culture loses the signal that design docs and proposal gates gave platform teams; visibility now needs high-touch 1-on-1 engagement the author admits may not scale. Shadow-IT risk if structured contribution paths don't exist.
- [Augmented Coding Weekly #48: Claude Fable 5, AI safety, and the rockstar developer problem](../../sources/2026-06-12-augmented-coding-weekly-48.md) — the 'rockstar AI problem': agents generate volume and solve novel problems at the cost of maintainability debt. 'It's okay to move slower, to ensure the software is high quality' — the open question is how teams operationalize that under velocity pressure.
- [Supervisory engineering: Orchestrating software's 'middle loop'](../../sources/2026-06-09-supervisory-engineering.md) — Annie Vella's research picked up by Thoughtworks — supervisory engineering as an empirically-derived new role category emerging from AI adoption
