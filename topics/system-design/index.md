# System design

How AI agents and AI-assisted tooling interact with system-level architectural decisions — what humans must own, where AI tools can assist, and the boundary between AI-generated proposals and human-approved architecture.

## Sources

- [AI sprinkled on top of a broken software lifecycle](../../sources/2026-05-31-ai-sprinkled-broken-software.md) — Opus/Sonnet useful for architecture sanity-checks; human owns system design decisions and risk acceptance; AI as advisor, not authority.
- [Harness Engineering Eras](../../sources/2026-06-05-harness-engineering-eras.md) — three-era AI system design framing (prompt → context → harness); harness design as the primary system design concern in the AI era; five structural components (personalisation, delegation, memory, context, action).
- [Packaging Copilot Agents & Skills with APM](../../sources/2026-06-13-packaging-github-copilot-apm.md) — "a package boundary is a way of deciding what should travel together and what should not" — drawing scenario-package lines is a platform-engineering design judgment, not just a packaging convenience.
- [Harness engineering: Codex in an agent-first world (OpenAI)](../../sources/2026-06-13-harness-engineering-codex.md) — the new system-design job is making constraints *legible and enforceable* to agents, not writing code. Entropy management is the unsolved problem: at agent-generation throughput, repository entropy grows faster than human capacity to manage it; human-written rules don't decay-correct at that speed.
- [grill-with-docs: Align Before You Build (Pocock)](../../sources/2026-06-10-grill-with-docs.md) — DDD applied to AI-assisted design: bounded contexts + ADRs capturing real trade-offs (ON DELETE RESTRICT vs CASCADE, cardinality choices). Record a decision only when it's hard to reverse or surprising without context — keeps the decision log lean and the schema intentional.
- [What Happens to Platform Teams?](../../sources/2026-06-15-platform-teams-ai-era.md) — APIs over products: expose lower-level composable primitives with non-negotiable guarantees (security, observability, compliance) at the foundation, instead of opinionated end-to-end products teams now route around.
- [Mark Richards and Neal Ford Reflect on Software Architecture: The Hard Parts](../../sources/2026-06-10-mark-richards-neal-ford-software-architecture-hard-parts.md) — retrospective on *Software Architecture: The Hard Parts* — architecture is competing forces with no clean recipes; distributed-system tradeoffs (consistency/availability, latency/correctness) resist formulaic answers. Notes the AI era wasn't covered in the 2021 original. [thin: partial auto-transcript]
- [AI/ML Engineer Skills Beyond Prompt Engineering](../../sources/2026-06-10-ai-ml-engineer-skills.md) — inference is foundational: KV-cache management, prefill vs decode latency, continuous batching, paged attention, speculative decoding, quantization (INT8/INT4/FP8/AWQ/GPTQ); plus GPU scheduling, KV-cache-aware autoscaling, multi-tenant isolation and cross-user context-contamination prevention.
- [Supervisory engineering: Orchestrating software's 'middle loop'](../../sources/2026-06-09-supervisory-engineering-middle-loop.md) — Architecture becomes executable constraint: supervisory engineers must codify design intent explicitly so agents don't hallucinate patterns or violate architecture
